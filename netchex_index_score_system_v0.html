Pillar Framework — Recommendation & Rationale
The four proposed pillars are retained with one strategic rename. "Save Headaches" becomes Reduce Risk — boardroom language that a CFO can quantify and a CEO can understand. All four pillars are restructured so each one leads with the CFO's cost lens, speaks to the HR Director's people lens, and surfaces the Office Manager's time lens simultaneously.
Pillar 01
Save Dollars
Hard cost reduction across overtime spend, payroll errors, supplemental payrolls, tax penalties, cost-per-hire, and turnover replacement costs. The CFO's native language — every gap translates directly to the P&L.
CFO: direct P&L line items HR: turnover cost visibility Mgr: OT approval control
Pillar 02
Save Time
Admin and manager hours recovered across payroll processing, benefits enrollment, onboarding, report-building, timecard approvals, and fielding employee questions. The Office Manager's most urgent felt pain.
CFO: labor cost per process HR: strategic capacity freed Mgr: hours back every week
Pillar 03
Drive Engagement
Employee experience uplift measured in retention rate, platform adoption, time-to-hire, time-to-productive, and engagement visibility. The HR Director's scorecard — and a CFO story when turnover costs are attached.
CFO: retention = cost avoidance HR: culture & talent density Mgr: new hire ramp speed
Pillar 04
Reduce Risk
Compliance exposure reduction across ACA filing, tax notices, benefits enrollment errors, audit readiness, and wage-and-hour exposure. Boardroom language that lands for all three personas — nobody wants to be the one who missed a filing.
CFO: penalty & audit exposure HR: compliance confidence Mgr: no surprise errors
Why these four pillars serve all three score goals

Expose unknown pain

Platform utilization rates, compliance confidence scores, and time-to-onboard benchmarks surface gaps the prospect has never measured — and their current platform has never shown them.

Validate felt pain

Overtime spend, admin hours, and payroll error frequency give language and dollar figures to problems they've lived with for years but couldn't quantify for leadership.

Differentiate from competitors

Each pillar has a dimension where ADP/Paychex, Paycom/Paylocity, and Rippling/Gusto each have a structural weakness — and Netchex has a measurable answer.

The rename rationale

"Reduce Risk" lands in a CFO's vocabulary. "Save Headaches" lands for an office manager. Since the report needs to go upstairs, risk framing earns the meeting — headache framing keeps it.

    Scoring Methodology
    How raw answers convert to sub-scores, a composite Netchex Index Score, benchmark deltas, and qualitative tier labels — with persona-weighted emphasis logic.

    Save Dollars — sub-score logic

    Overtime %Weight: 25%
    Tax penalties (2yr)Weight: 20%
    Payroll error rateWeight: 20%
    Turnover costWeight: 20%
    Cost-per-hire vs. benchmarkWeight: 15%
    Score = 100 − (weighted penalty points). Penalty points assigned per metric by distance from best-in-class benchmark for their industry + size cohort.

    Save Time — sub-score logic

    Admin hrs/wk payrollWeight: 25%
    Admin hrs/wk benefits + Q&AWeight: 20%
    Manager hrs/wk onboardingWeight: 20%
    Time-to-hire (days)Weight: 20%
    Manual data re-entryWeight: 15%
    Score = 100 − (weighted penalty points). Each hour above benchmark costs 3 points. Manual re-entry = automatic 15-point penalty regardless of hours reported.

    Drive Engagement — sub-score logic

    Annual turnover rateWeight: 30%
    Platform utilization %Weight: 25%
    Engagement measured (Y/N)Weight: 20%
    Manager visibility scoreWeight: 15%
    First paycheck speedWeight: 10%
    Not measuring engagement = automatic 20-point cap on this sub-score (max 80). Platform utilization below 40% = 15-point penalty.

    Reduce Risk — sub-score logic

    Compliance self-rating (1–5)Weight: 25%
    ACA/DOL issues (2yr)Weight: 30%
    Benefits enrollment errorsWeight: 25%
    Audit/wage dispute historyWeight: 20%
    Any confirmed audit, DOL inquiry, or wage-and-hour dispute in 2 years = automatic "At Risk" floor on this pillar regardless of other scores.
    Composite Score & Tier Labels
    Composite formula
    Netchex Index Score = weighted average of four sub-scores:
    Save Dollars × 0.30 + Save Time × 0.25 + Drive Engagement × 0.25 + Reduce Risk × 0.20

    Dollar and Time pillars carry higher weight because they produce quantifiable ROI for CFO presentations. Persona weighting (below) can re-order emphasis in the report narrative without changing the score.
    Tier thresholds
    0–44At Risk
    45–64Average
    65–79Strong
    80–100Best-in-Class
    Tiers apply to each pillar sub-score independently AND to the composite. A prospect can be "Strong" overall but "At Risk" on a single pillar — that's the most powerful coaching scenario.
    Persona-Weighted Report Emphasis

    CFO in the room

    Lead report with Save Dollars pillar. Translate all time gaps into dollar equivalents (hrs × blended rate). Frame engagement as turnover cost avoidance. Risk pillar = penalty + audit exposure.

    HR Director in the room

    Lead with Drive Engagement pillar. Frame Save Dollars as "investing in people pays back." Translate risk as compliance confidence. Time savings = strategic capacity for culture work.

    Office Manager in the room

    Lead with Save Time pillar. Speak in hours per week, not abstract percentages. Frame risk as "no more surprise errors." Engagement = faster new hire ramp, fewer repeat questions.

    "I don't know" protocol

    Auto-apply industry average for scoring. Flag each IDK answer as an AE coaching moment with this framing: "The fact that your current platform doesn't make this visible to you is part of what we're here to solve."
