The Objective

Our North Star

"Make the decision maker feel that purchasing Netchex is how they become a hero in their boss's eyes."

The buyer is not just evaluating software. They are evaluating whether they can walk into their CFO's or CEO's office and say: "I found us a solution that will measurably improve our business." The Netchex Index Score gives them that moment — backed by data, industry benchmarks, and a boardroom-ready report they can hand upstairs without modification.

The Methodology

Scoring & Tiers

How raw answers convert to sub-scores, a composite Netchex Index Score, benchmark deltas, and qualitative tier labels — with persona-adaptive report emphasis.

Save Dollars — sub-score weights
Overtime % of payroll25%
Tax penalties (2yr)20%
Payroll error rate20%
Turnover replacement cost20%
Cost-per-hire vs. benchmark15%
Score = 100 − weighted penalty points. Penalties assigned per distance from best-in-class benchmark for industry + size cohort.
Save Time — sub-score weights
Admin hrs/wk — payroll25%
Admin hrs/wk — benefits + Q&A20%
Manager hrs/wk — onboarding20%
Time-to-hire (days)20%
Manual data re-entry15%
Each hour above benchmark costs 3 points. Manual re-entry = automatic 15-point penalty regardless of hours reported.
Drive Engagement — sub-score weights
Annual turnover rate30%
Platform utilization %25%
Engagement measured (Y/N)20%
Manager visibility score15%
First paycheck speed10%
Not measuring engagement = automatic 20-point cap (max 80). Platform utilization below 40% = 15-point penalty.
Reduce Risk — sub-score weights
Compliance self-rating (1–5)25%
ACA / DOL issues (2yr)30%
Benefits enrollment errors25%
Audit / wage dispute history20%
Any confirmed audit, DOL inquiry, or wage-and-hour dispute in 2 years = automatic At Risk floor on this pillar.

Composite score formula

Netchex Index Score =
Save Dollars × 0.30
+ Save Time × 0.25
+ Drive Engagement × 0.25
+ Reduce Risk × 0.20
Dollar and Time pillars carry higher composite weight because they produce quantifiable ROI for CFO presentations. Persona weighting can re-order report narrative emphasis without changing the score itself.

Tier thresholds — per pillar & composite

0 – 44At Risk
45 – 64Average
65 – 79Strong
80 – 100Best-in-Class
Tiers apply to each pillar sub-score independently AND to the composite. A prospect can be "Strong" overall but "At Risk" on a single pillar — that is the most powerful coaching scenario.

Persona-weighted report emphasis

CFO in the room
Lead report with Save Dollars pillar. Translate all time gaps into dollar equivalents. Frame engagement as turnover cost avoidance. Risk = penalty + audit exposure.
HR Director in the room
Lead with Drive Engagement pillar. Frame Save Dollars as "investing in people pays back." Translate risk as compliance confidence. Time savings = strategic capacity.
Office Manager in the room
Lead with Save Time pillar. Speak in hours per week, not percentages. Frame risk as "no more surprise errors." Engagement = faster new hire ramp.

The Data Layer

Benchmark Assumptions

Default benchmarks across hospitality, auto dealerships, and healthcare. All figures are estimates based on SHRM, BLS, and published industry reports — to be updated annually and validated against Netchex customer data.

Data sources: SHRM Benchmarking Reports (2023–2024), BLS Occupational Employment & Wage Statistics, DHI-DFH Mean Vacancy Duration, National Restaurant Association workforce data, NADA annual dealership workforce study, SHRM Healthcare HR research. All figures represent industry averages, not Netchex customer data. Best-in-class benchmarks represent approximately top-quartile performance for companies of 100–500 employees in each sector. All figures should be treated as estimates and validated before use in client-facing materials.
